Q)   How do you bootstrap the first privileged user (i.e. the super-admin)?
Ans) This is the classic industry problem and the solution is Super Admin is not registered through the app at all. We create the Super Admin directly in the database — manually, one time, before the app goes live. A seed script basically. Steps are: Takes the super admin's email + password --> Hashes the password (Imp) --> Inserts directly into the users table with role = "super_admin"

Q)   Why not take 'googleId' as primary key instead of 'id' ?
Ans) Due to Performance: 'id' is a serial integer — small, fast to index and join on. 'googleId' is a 21 character string — slower for joins, especially when your group_members table references it as foreign key.


*  If you need to check whether rows were affected after insert, update or delete, always use  .returning()
*  Searching an indexed column across many rows is faster than scanning an array in one row. This is literally why relational databases were invented. Indexes on foreign keys are one of PostgreSQL's core strengths.  And that is the reason why we chose group_id as foreign key in my group_members table instead of an array of group_members_id in our groups table. With group_id in members table — PostgreSQL puts an index on that foreign key. Finding all members of a group is an instant indexed lookup.

** Important Game Flow: My flow is that cards of sky, earth, sun and moon genre are distributed among candidates. Now instead of a single person registering genre of each candidate in the db, we will let the candidate themselves register their genre, but with a security check. What if A got sky and B got sun, But both A and B wants sky, so A registers himself as sky only and B also register itself as sky, that's cheating. So for security, what we will do is, assign four volunteers, each having a QR of a respective genre. Now when A goes to register sky, the volunter checks that whether his card is actually sky, if not then the volunteer wont let him scan the QR only, and then lets him scan the sky QR, and then it itself registers A under sky category. Now what if A scans the sky QR, goes back and give the same card to B to register him as sky too. The best solution would have been, to give a unique number for each card that is once used cannot be reused, but let's say cards are already made, so it is not possible now, so instead what we can do is once A scans his card, he would be isolated from the other participants who have not registered themselves yet, so that card cannot be reused  

** Now there is one problem, assigning each member their genre via a db call, then on group creation, there is a db call, then again for assigning each member a db call to check if not more than 1 of each genre is there and then finally a db to add group and member. thats a lot of db call yrr. 
So i am thinking to use redis instead, like members assigned their genre, and that record stays in redis now (no db call yet). A group is ceated with no member now, a unique code for that group is created with a TTL that lives in redis. each partcipant enter the group id before time expires (if all of them did not register before the TTL, the the group is totally removed, and has to be re-created, even removing the participants that has been registered successfully). Now my backend and redis will work together, lets demosntrate with an example. lets say, A, B, C, D are forming a group. Each one goes to their respective section to register their genre, now the id of the person and the assigned genre remains in db now. A group is created with id 123 and TTL of 30 sec. Everone, expect for the one who created group, will have to enter the group id within 30 sec. If all 4 has done, then group created in db with all members. Lets say, B, C got register but D could not complete it on time, so after 30 sec all of A, B, C, D registartion are cancelled, and they have to re-create the group. now what happen in my redis, it will wait till 30 sec or till all 4 members have entered the same group code. Once it recives all 4 members requesting to join a group, it will check from redis itself that whether all 4 member who requested to join the same group belong to same genre or not, if any one fails, entire registartion is eliminated get and group id also get cleared up. if it passes successfully, then it finally sends all details to backend, group name, particpants id, and bakend makes a final db call to store these values, and now the A, B, C, D gets cleared up from db, only after getting registred in db, and group details also get cleared up from db after registartion. Also we will put TTL for group id, but not for member after they register their genre, coz finding a team might take time for some.
This technique is called 'optimistic temporary state with atomic commit' — store everything in Redis temporarily, validate, then commit to DB in one shot only on success.
